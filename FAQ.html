What percentage of cocoa is in each slab?

Original & Silk variations contain 75% cocoa solids. I also offer a custom bar where you can choose your own percentage to suit your taste buds (just drop me a message!).

What size/weight are the slabs?

75g+. As they are all handmade and hand-poured, sometimes I overpour! (woops)

Is your chocolate “Bean-to-bar”?

Slabs chocolate is made from scratch using organic and fair-trade raw ingredients. As SLABS is a (very!) small start-up business, I use cacao mass rather than buying whole cocoa beans. This eliminates the need for expensive machinery, whilst ensuring that I am in full control of what ingredients go in each slab. I create each recipe (choosing the ratios of cocoa) and hand-temper each bar (the process which gets the chocolate to a stable and shiny state!), and add any flavours e.g. fruit and nuts.

Um.. what is cocoa mass?

Whole cocoa beans are roasted, winnowed (a process to remove the shells), and ground into a paste. This is what creates “mass”, sometimes known as “cocoa liquor”.

What cocoa do you use?

I use Criollo cocoa from the finest Peruvian Criollo beans. Criollo is well-known for being the “premium” bean and is one of the most celebrated fine cocoa varieties. This was very important in the development of SLABS to only use the finest ingredients, as the type of cocoa is fundamental to the taste and texture of the chocolate. As I don’t process the chocolate in machinery, the bars have slightly more texture than commercial, mass-produced bars.

How are you different from other artisan chocolate retailers?

Firstly, all ingredients are ethically sourced and 100% organic. This was also really important in developing Slabs and keeping things premium and fair. My cocoa is sourced from small farms in Peru who’s workers are passionate about quality and are paid properly for their work.

Secondly, most chocolatiers buy couverture (ready-made chocolate), add a pinch of salt and sell it on for a profit. This means their chocolate could contain any number of bad fats, extra sugar, or artificial flavourings to cover up the fact their cocoa might be lower in quality or make their manufacturing costs cheaper.

In contrast, SLABS makes the chocolate from scratch which means you get the highest quality and lowest processing as possible. All bars are free from soy lecithin, palm oil and absolutely anything artificial.

What's wrong with soy and palm oil?

These ingredients are used in nearly everything, so soy and palm oil crops are grown in vast amounts to keep up with demand. This means that rainforests are being cut down to make way for soy and palm oil plantations, resulting in many animals being on the verge of extinction due to losing their natural habitats (which we definitely don’t want).

Soy is used as an emulsifier which makes it easier to process, while palm oil is cheaper and pads out the chocolate in place of the cocoa butter. Real chocolate needs neither of these things! So I left it out. Let’s save the rainforests.

Do you have a low-sugar version?

My Original and Silk variations are already lower in sugar than your average Bournville or milk chocolates as dark chocolate has less sugar (the higher the cocoa %, the lower the sugar %). If you want darker, I’d be happy to make you a custom bar to whatever percentage you like! Personally I love 85%.

Are your products free from allergens?

I can’t guarantee all bars are free from nut traces, as they are created in the same space where nuts are used (for my cashew bars).
